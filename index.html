What is a haiku?

Haiku is an ancient poetic form that captures a brief moment in time. Just three lines long, a haiku is traditionally inspired by nature, but modern poets use the form for many other topics. Haiku originated in Japan, where there are many great masters of the form.

How to write a haiku

A haiku consists of three lines. The first two lines often contain imagery, and the third may contain a surprise.
