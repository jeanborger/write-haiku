What is a haiku?

Haiku is an ancient poetic form that strives to capture a brief, fleeting moment in time. It is just three lines long. Traditionally, haiku is inspired by nature, but modern poets have used the form to discuss many other topics. Haiku originated in Japan, and there are several great Japanese masters of the form.

How do I write a haiku?

A haiku consists of three lines. The first two lines often contain imagery, and the third may contain a surprise.
